A gooseneck kettle is the invisible hand behind every great pour over. Standard kettles dump water in an uncontrollable gush — fine for making tea, useless for the slow, deliberate pour that V60 and Kalita Wave demand. The narrow, curved spout of a gooseneck gives you pinpoint control over flow rate and placement, turning a chaotic splash into a precise stream the width of a pencil.

If you're serious about pour over, this is the upgrade that changes everything.

## Why Gooseneck? Can't I Just Use a Regular Kettle?

You can. But you'll fight it every brew. Pour over recipes call for specific pour rates — typically 3-5 ml per second during the bloom, ramping up to 7-10 ml/s during the main pour. A standard kettle with a wide spout makes this nearly impossible. You'll overshoot your target weight, create uneven saturation, and produce inconsistent cups.

A gooseneck spout restricts flow naturally, so you control speed by tilting angle alone. It's the difference between painting with a roller and painting with a fine brush.

## Electric vs Stovetop: Which Type Do You Need?

### Electric Gooseneck Kettles ($60-$200)

Electric gooseneck kettles heat water and hold it at your target temperature — no thermometer, no guesswork. The best models let you set temperature to the degree and hold it for 30-60 minutes. This is a genuine workflow improvement: set your temp, grind your coffee, and the water is waiting at exactly 93°C when you're ready.

**Best for**: Daily pour over brewers, anyone who values precision and convenience, people who brew multiple cups throughout the day.

### Stovetop Gooseneck Kettles ($25-$50)

Stovetop models are simpler and cheaper. Heat them on any burner, use a thermometer (or the off-boil timing method) to hit your target temperature. No electronics means nothing to break, and they work on gas, electric, and induction cooktops.

**Best for**: Budget-conscious brewers, minimalists, anyone who already owns a good thermometer, camping and off-grid use.

## What to Look For When Buying

### Spout Design

The spout is everything. Look for a narrow, gradually tapered spout that allows both slow drips and faster pours. The best spouts let you pour as slowly as 2 ml/s and as fast as 15 ml/s by changing your tilt angle alone.

Avoid kettles with abruptly narrowed spouts — they create turbulence and dripping.

### Temperature Control Precision

For electric kettles, 1°C precision is the standard. Anything coarser (5°C steps, for example) limits your ability to fine-tune brewing for different roast levels. As we covered in our water temperature guide, even a 3°C difference can shift a cup from sour to sweet.

### Hold Mode Duration

A 30-60 minute hold mode is essential for electric kettles. It keeps your water at the target temperature while you grind, prep, or get distracted by email. Without it, you're re-boiling every time you step away — wasting energy and time.

### Capacity

- **0.6-0.8L**: Perfect for single cups (V60, AeroPress)
- **0.9-1.0L**: Ideal for most home brewers (1-3 cups)
- **1.2L+**: Best for Chemex or serving multiple people

### Handle Ergonomics

You'll hold this kettle for 2-3 minutes per brew, pouring with precision the entire time. A comfortable, well-balanced handle reduces wrist fatigue and improves pour consistency. Counterbalanced handles (like the Stagg EKG's) keep the kettle feeling light even when full.
