Pour over brewing is the purest expression of coffee craft. You, hot water, ground coffee, and gravity — nothing more. But the dripper you choose shapes everything about the cup in your hand, from clarity and sweetness to body and complexity.

## Why Pour Over?

Pour over gives you total control. Unlike automatic machines, you decide the water temperature, pour rate, agitation, and timing. This means you can dial in a recipe that highlights exactly what you love about a particular coffee. Light-roasted Ethiopian with blueberry notes? Pour over will reveal every nuance.

## How to Choose a Dripper

### Cone vs Flat Bottom

Cone drippers like the Hario V60 channel water through a single point, creating a faster, more dynamic extraction. Flat-bottom drippers like the Kalita Wave spread water evenly across the bed, producing a more consistent and forgiving brew. Beginners often find flat-bottom drippers easier to work with.

### Material Matters

- **Ceramic**: Best heat retention, ideal for consistent extraction temperature
- **Glass**: Beautiful, good heat retention, fragile
- **Stainless Steel**: Durable, travel-friendly, heats up quickly
- **Plastic**: Lightweight, surprisingly good thermal properties, unbreakable
- **Copper**: Premium heat conductivity, develops patina over time

## Our Top Picks

### Best Overall: Hario V60 02 Ceramic ($29)

The V60 is the undisputed king of pour over. Its spiral ribs create air channels between the filter and dripper wall, while the large single hole gives you complete control over flow rate. The 02 size handles 1-4 cups perfectly, making it the most versatile option.

The learning curve is real — the V60 punishes sloppy technique — but once you dial it in, nothing else comes close for clarity and sweetness.

### Best for Beginners: Kalita Wave 185 ($38)

If the V60 is a sports car, the Wave is a luxury sedan. Its flat bottom and three small drain holes produce an incredibly consistent cup regardless of your pour technique. The patented Wave filters prevent the coffee bed from touching the dripper walls, ensuring even extraction every time.

### Best for Entertaining: Chemex Classic 6-Cup ($48)

The Chemex is as much sculpture as brewer. Its proprietary bonded filters are 20-30% thicker than standard pour over filters, removing more oils and producing an exceptionally clean, bright cup. The 6-cup size is perfect for brewing for guests, and the hourglass design looks stunning on any counter.

### Best Hybrid: Hario Switch 02 ($35)

Can't decide between pour over and immersion? The Switch gives you both. Steep your coffee like a French press, then flip the switch to drain through a V60 filter. It's incredibly versatile and nearly foolproof — steep for 2 minutes, switch, done.

### Best Premium: Hario V60 Copper ($80)

For the serious home barista who wants the best thermal performance, the copper V60 offers superior heat conductivity. It maintains brewing temperature throughout the pour better than any other material. Plus, it develops a beautiful patina with use that tells the story of every cup you've brewed.

## Essential Pour Over Accessories

A great dripper is just the start. You'll also need:

- **Gooseneck kettle** for precise pouring (Fellow Stagg EKG recommended)
- **Coffee scale** with timer for consistency (Timemore Black Mirror works great)
- **Quality grinder** set to medium-fine (Comandante C40 for hand grinding)
- **Fresh, specialty-grade coffee** roasted within 2-4 weeks

## The Bottom Line

Start with the Kalita Wave 185 if you want reliability, or the Hario V60 02 if you want to develop your craft. Either way, pour over will transform your relationship with coffee.
